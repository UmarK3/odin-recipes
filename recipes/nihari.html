Nihari

Lamb Shank Nihari

Nihari is a rich, slow-cooked meat stew that is a hallmark of the Mughlai and Awadhi cuisines of the Indian subcontinent, specifically tracing its origins back to 18th-century Delhi and Lucknow. The word itself comes from the Arabic word 'Nahar' (نهار), meaning morning, reflecting its original function: a hearty, high-energy morning meal. Traditionally, it was prepared overnight and eaten by Muslim noblemen after the morning prayer (Fajr), often followed by a nap until the mid-day prayer. Over time, it also became a staple for working-class labourers, who relied on the nutrient-dense dish, to sustain them through long, cold days. Today, Nihari is revered as a celebratory dish, particularly during Eid (much like Biryani), and is cherished for its depth of flavour, velvety-thick gravy, and meltingly tender meat, often served with warm, pillowy naan.

Ingredients

Core Components

  • Lamb Shanks
  • Ghee/Oil
  • Water

Aromatics

  • Onion
  • Garlic-Garlic paste

Nihari Masala

  • Fennel Seeds
  • Cumin Seeds
  • Corriander Seeds
  • Peppercorns
  • Cloves
  • Star Anise
  • Bay Leaves
  • Red Chilli Powder
  • Tumeric Powder
  • Salt

Thickener

  • Pain Flour (Atta)
  • Water

Garnish

  • Ginger
  • Green Chilli
  • Corriander
  • Lemon/Lime

Steps

Part 1: Prepare the Masala

  1. Toast whole spices - gently toast fennel seeds, cumin seeds, coriander seeds, peppercorns, cloves, green cardamom, star anise and bay leaves.
  2. Grind - after 2 minutes grind the spices into a fine powder.
  3. Mix - combine the spice blend with red chilli powder, tumeric powder and salt.

Part 2: Sear the Meat

  1. Brown Shanks - heat the oil over medium-heat and place lamb shanks until nicely brown.
  2. Saute Onions - within the same pot add sliced onions and fry for about 8-10 minutes.
  3. Add Aromatics - stir in the ginger-garlic paste and cook for 2 minutes.
  4. Bloom Spices - add the prepared masala to the pot and fry the spice for 1-2 minutes. Add water to prevent burning.

Part 3: Slow Cook

  1. Add Meat and water - return lamb shanks to the pot and add water to cover the shanks, bring to a boil.
  2. Simmer - once boiling, reduce to lowest heat and cover pot with a lid.
  3. Cook for Tenderness - simmer for 3-4 hours until meat is tender; falling off the bone.

Part 4: Thicken and Finish

  1. Prepare slurry - once meat is tender mix plain flour with cup of cold water to form a paste.
  2. Thicken Gravy - slowly pour slurry into the Nihari and stir consistently. Allow the stew to simmer for another 10-15 minutes until gravy is thickened.

Part 5: Garnish and Serve

  1. Serve - ladle the hot nihari, ensuring each bowl has a lamb shank and plenty of gravy.
  2. Garnish - Sprinkle ginger, chopped gree chillies, corriander and a squeeze of lemon juice.
  3. Enjoy with naan, tandoori roti or even plain rice.
